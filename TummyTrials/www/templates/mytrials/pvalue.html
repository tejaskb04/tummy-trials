P-value
What is a p-value?

The p-value of your study represents the probability of the result being true.

How to interpret the p-value:

  • p-value less than 0.05 is a strong evidence
  • p-value between 0.05 and 0.15 is a possible evidence
  • p-value between 0.15 and 0.35 is a weak evidence
  • p-value more than 0.35 is no evidence

example: A p-value of 0.06 means there is possible evidence that the trigger you are testing in the trial can be causing your symptoms.
